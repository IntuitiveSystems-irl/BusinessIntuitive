What is a DSCR loan?

A DSCR loan is an investment-property mortgage qualified by the rental income of the property — not the borrower's personal income. DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio. It compares the property's effective rental income against the total monthly housing payment, called PITIA (Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + Association dues).

Rule of thumb: a DSCR at or above 1.25 is typically viewed as strong. 1.00–1.25 is workable but tighter. Below 1.00 usually needs restructuring — bigger down payment, lower payment, or stronger rent comps.

Frequently asked questions

Everything investors ask about DSCR loans, property investment financing, and the path to a faster close.

What is a DSCR loan?

A DSCR loan is an investment-property mortgage that qualifies the borrower based on the rental income of the property rather than personal income. DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio — the ratio between the property's expected rental income and its total monthly housing payment (PITIA). Most DSCR lenders look for a ratio of 1.00–1.25 or higher. DSCR loans are popular with real estate investors because they don't require W-2 income, tax returns, or traditional debt-to-income calculations.

How do I calculate DSCR?

DSCR = Effective Monthly Rent ÷ PITIA, where PITIA is Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + HOA. Effective rent is gross rent reduced by a vacancy estimate (commonly 5%).

Example: $2,400 rent at 5% vacancy and $1,800 PITIA → $2,280 ÷ $1,800 = 1.27 DSCR. Or just use the free DSCR Quick Check calculator at the top of this page.

What is a good DSCR ratio for an investment property?

A DSCR of 1.25 or higher is generally considered strong and qualifies for the best DSCR loan rates. 1.00–1.24 is workable but tight — many lenders will approve but may require larger reserves, higher down payment, or stronger credit. Below 1.00 usually requires restructuring: bigger down payment, lower purchase price, or higher rent assumption supported by comps.

Do DSCR loans require personal income verification?

No. The defining feature of a DSCR loan is that it qualifies based on the property's rental income, not the borrower's personal income. You typically do not need W-2s, pay stubs, or tax returns. Lenders will still verify credit score, reserves, and the property's appraised rent — but personal debt-to-income ratio is not the qualifying factor.

What is PITIA?

PITIA stands for Principal, Interest, Taxes, Insurance, and Association dues (HOA). It represents the full monthly housing payment a DSCR lender uses when calculating Debt Service Coverage Ratio. PITIA is the denominator in the DSCR equation: DSCR = Rent ÷ PITIA.

What credit score do I need for a DSCR loan?

Most DSCR lenders require a minimum FICO of 660–680, with the best rates and terms reserved for borrowers above 740. Some intelligent lenders flex to 620 with stronger DSCR, larger reserves, or higher down payment. Credit score affects rate, leverage (LTV), and reserve requirements more than it affects approval/denial.

How much down payment is required for a DSCR loan?

DSCR loan down payments typically range from 20% to 25% for a single-family rental, and 25%–30% for multi-unit or short-term rental properties. Stronger DSCR ratios and credit scores can reduce the required down payment. Cash-out refinances generally cap at 70–75% LTV.

Can I get a DSCR loan in an LLC?

Yes — DSCR loans are commonly closed in the name of an LLC, which is one of the reasons real estate investors prefer them. The LLC needs to be in good standing with a current operating agreement, EIN confirmation letter, and consistent signers across title, banking, and the loan documents. Personal guarantees from the members are usually still required.

What reserves do DSCR lenders require?

Most DSCR lenders require 6 to 12 months of PITIA in liquid reserves at closing. The amount depends on credit score, DSCR ratio, and number of properties owned. Reserves must be documented with two months of statements, with any large deposits sourced and seasoned. Investors with multiple financed properties often face higher reserve requirements per property.

What is an intelligent lender for property investors?

An intelligent lender is one who actually understands the structure of investor deals — DSCR products, entity structures, BRRRR strategies, short-term rental income calculation, and the practical constraints of real estate investing. They communicate clearly, pre-stage documents, give realistic timelines, and don't waste your time on products that won't fit your scenario. The opposite is a generic mortgage shop that treats every investor like a residential borrower and stalls deals over preventable issues.

How long does a DSCR loan take to close?

A well-prepared DSCR loan typically closes in 21 to 30 days. Closings stretch to 45–60 days when documentation is delivered piecemeal, when entity paperwork is inconsistent, or when the appraisal flags rent comp issues. Investors who pre-stage their reserves documentation, entity docs, lease comps, and insurance quote routinely close on the faster end.

Can I use a DSCR loan for short-term rentals (Airbnb / VRBO)?

Yes. Many DSCR lenders accept short-term rental income, but the methodology varies. Some use 12 months of actual STR income (AirDNA, host platform reports). Others use long-term rental comps regardless of how the property is operated. STR-friendly DSCR products usually require slightly higher reserves and may cap LTV 5% lower than long-term rental products. Always confirm the lender's STR methodology before applying.

What's the difference between DSCR and DTI?

DTI (Debt-to-Income) is a personal-income metric used in conventional mortgages — it compares the borrower's monthly debt payments to their gross monthly income. DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) is a property-income metric used in investor loans — it compares the property's rental income to its housing payment. DTI requires W-2s and tax returns; DSCR does not. Real estate investors generally prefer DSCR because it scales with their portfolio without exhausting personal DTI capacity.
