How to sell web systems to contractors

Sell web systems to contractors by leading with the problem (lost leads, no booking, weak online trust), not the product. The process: identify no-site or weak-site contractors, make a short call, book a 10-minute walkthrough, show real examples, present a clear package ($2k–$5k+), and close with a checkout link.

The 7-step sales process

This is the process used by Business Intuitive sales reps through the XO Dispatch platform. It is designed for new reps to be productive on their first day.

1Identify no-site or weak-site contractors

Start with contractors who have no website or a clearly outdated one. The problem is visible — you do not need the prospect to agree something is wrong because you can show them. XO Dispatch surfaces these leads automatically based on website analysis.

2Lead with the problem, not the product

Open with what they are losing: "I noticed you don't have a website, which probably means you're losing jobs to competitors who show up when people search for [their trade] in [their area]." Do not open with what you sell.

3Use a structured call script

Follow a decision-tree script that adapts based on the contractor's responses. This keeps new reps consistent, prevents rambling, and handles common objections (price, timing, "I'll think about it") with pre-built branches.

4Book a 10-minute walkthrough

The goal of the first call is not to close. It is to book a short walkthrough where you show the contractor what a system looks like for their business specifically. This is the primary conversion event — everything before it is setup.

5Show, do not tell

During the walkthrough, show real examples: lead capture forms, booking widgets, mobile optimization, Google review integration, automated follow-up sequences. Contractors respond to concrete demonstrations, not abstract pitches.

6Present a clear package

Offer a defined package with clear deliverables: website, booking system, follow-up automation, CRM setup. Typical packages range from $2,000 to $5,000+. Avoid custom quoting on the first conversation — defined packages reduce decision fatigue.

7Close with a checkout link

Generate a checkout link and send it directly. Keep the buying process as simple as possible. The fewer steps between "yes" and payment, the higher the close rate.

Why this works: 3 reasons

  1. The problem is visible — You are not selling something abstract. A missing or broken website is something the contractor can see. This makes the conversation practical instead of theoretical.
  2. The first ask is small — "Can I show you what this would look like for your business?" is an easy yes. You are not asking for money on the first call — just 10 minutes.
  3. The solution is concrete — You are showing a website, a booking system, and automated follow-up. These are tangible things the contractor can understand immediately.

Common objections and how to handle them

  • "How much does it cost?" — "It depends on what you need, but our packages start around $2-3k. The best thing is to let me show you what it looks like for your business — takes about 10 minutes."
  • "I'll think about it" — "Totally fair. While you're thinking, can I send you the example so you have something concrete to look at?" (Send the walkthrough link.)
  • "I already have a website" — "I saw that — I actually pulled it up. Can I show you two things that might be costing you leads right now? It'll take 2 minutes."
  • "I get all my work from referrals" — "That's great — it means your work speaks for itself. A web system doesn't replace referrals, it captures the people who hear about you and then Google your name."
