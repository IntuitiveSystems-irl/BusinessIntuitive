What is a Strategic Systems Diagnostic?

A Strategic Systems Diagnostic is a structured assessment that maps a business's operational blind spots across four dimensions — strategy, systems, operations, and growth — to reveal where revenue is leaking due to structural inefficiencies. It is designed for businesses generating $1M–$10M+ that are growing revenue but losing margin they cannot explain.

Unlike a standard business audit, the diagnostic does not just look at what is broken. It examines the connections between systems — how your sales process feeds into operations, how your tools interact, where handoffs break down, and where structural gaps cause revenue to leak silently.

The 4 dimensions of a Strategic Systems Diagnostic

1. Strategy alignment

Is your growth direction matched to your operational capacity? Many businesses pursue revenue targets that their current systems cannot support, creating invisible overhead that erodes margin.

2. Systems architecture

Are your tools, platforms, and workflows connected or creating friction? Disconnected systems force manual workarounds, duplicate data entry, and create gaps where information — and revenue — falls through.

3. Operational throughput

Are you converting inputs (leads, time, capital) into outputs (revenue, jobs, growth) efficiently? A 10% throughput leak on $2M revenue is $200K lost annually — usually without anyone noticing.

4. Growth bottlenecks

What is the single constraint preventing your next revenue tier? Most businesses have one primary bottleneck that, when removed, unlocks disproportionate growth. The diagnostic identifies it.

How a Strategic Systems Diagnostic works: 3 phases

  1. Discovery (week 1) — We map your current business structure: sales pipeline, operations, team, tools, and financial flow. This reveals the full picture of how the business actually runs versus how it is supposed to run.
  2. Analysis (weeks 2–3) — We run the four-dimension assessment to identify where revenue leaks occur, rank them by impact, and trace root causes. This is where the invisible problems become visible.
  3. Action plan (week 3–4) — We deliver a prioritized roadmap showing exactly which structural fixes will recover the most revenue, ranked by impact and implementation speed. No vague recommendations — specific actions with expected outcomes.

5 signs you need a Strategic Systems Diagnostic

  1. Revenue is growing but margins are shrinking — You are making more but keeping less. This almost always indicates a structural leak in operations or delivery.
  2. The team is growing but output is not proportional — Adding people should multiply capacity. If it does not, the systems are absorbing the extra capacity as overhead.
  3. Marketing spend is up but lead quality is declining — More money in, worse leads out. This points to misalignment between your growth strategy and your market positioning.
  4. The same problems keep returning — You fix something, it comes back 3 months later. This means you are treating symptoms, not the structural root cause.
  5. You know something is wrong but cannot pinpoint it — This is the most common sign. The problem is real but invisible because it lives in the connections between systems, not in any single system.

Who is a Strategic Systems Diagnostic for?

The diagnostic is designed for contractors, home service businesses, and trade professionals generating $1M–$10M+ in annual revenue. These businesses are past the startup phase — they have revenue, a team, and systems in place — but are hitting ceilings they cannot explain. The diagnostic finds the structural cause.
