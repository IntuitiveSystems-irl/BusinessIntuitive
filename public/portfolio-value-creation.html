03 — Proof

A representative engagement.

● Representative operational build

Field-services operator · LMM buyout portfolio · example of the type of build we scope

The situation

A field-services business was growing but operationally opaque. Manual reporting, quoting in spreadsheets, job status scattered across multiple disconnected tools, and no real-time visibility into margin by job or crew — the deal team's picture was always a month behind.

What we built (7 weeks)

  • A live operations dashboard: revenue, backlog, margin by job & crew
  • Quote-and-bid automation replacing the spreadsheet process
  • An internal job-tracking tool that retired 5 disconnected tools
  • An auto-generated monthly board & lender reporting pack

Reporting

Board and lender-monitoring cycle shortened. Packets assembled automatically — no manual assembly.

Admin overhead

Back-office labor significantly reduced through automation. Removed from the critical path.

Quoting & bids

Standardized with bid automation. Multi-day turnarounds replaced by a consistent, repeatable workflow.

Tool consolidation

Multiple disconnected tools replaced with one operating system. Single source of truth for the deal team.

What the deal team got: clean monitoring data, lower admin drag on EBITDA, and a more scalable and operationally mature company at exit. No new headcount. Fixed scope.
